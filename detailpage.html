Haarlem Oude Stad is known for its well-preserved historic center, which boasts a stunning array of medieval and Renaissance architecture. The city's layout, with its narrow cobbled streets and winding canals, reflects its centuries-old heritage.

The heart of Haarlem's Oude Stad is the Grote Markt (Great Market Square). This picturesque square is surrounded by beautiful historical buildings, including the impressive St. Bavo Church (Grote Kerk) and the Town Hall (Stadhuis). The Grote Markt is also home to a vibrant market and various cafes and restaurants.

Molen De Adriaan is a historic windmill located in Haarlem, Netherlands. It is a well-known landmark and tourist attraction in the city. The windmill was originally built in 1779 but was destroyed by fire in 1932. However, it was subsequently reconstructed in the 21st century and has been open to the public since 2002.

Teylers Museum is one of the oldest museums in the Netherlands and is celebrated for its unique blend of art, science, and historical artifacts. The museum's collections include paintings, fossils, scientific instruments, and rare books. The neoclassical architecture of the building itself is a sight to see.
